2007 Workshop on Scheme and Functional Programming

September 30th, 2007
Freiburg, Germany

Affiliated with ICFP 2007.

Purpose

The purpose of the workshop is to discuss experience with and future developments of the Scheme programming language, as well as general aspects of computer science loosely centered on the general theme of Scheme.


Programme and proceedings

The workshop happened on September 30th, 2007.  As many as 44 persons attended the workshop.


Registration

It is now possible to register via the ICFP web site.


Information about the poster session
  • There will be at least one pin board per poster presentation.

  • The poster session will possibly happen in a different room than the workshop.

Submission guidelines
  • Submissions should be sent to the program chair as a PDF or Postscript file that is printable on US Letter sized paper.
  • They should be no more than 12 pages (including bibliography and appendices).  Authors wishing to supply additional material to the reviewers beyond the 12 page limit can do so in clearly marked appendices, on the understanding that reviewers may not read the appendices.
  • They should be prepared in the standard ACM format: two columns, nine point font on ten point baseline, page 20pc (3.33in) wide and 54pc (9in) tall with a column gutter of 2pc (0.33in).
  • A LaTeX style is available: a suitable LaTex class, an accompanying guide, and a template.
  • Submitted papers must have content that has not previously been published in other conferences or refereed venues, and simultaneous submission to other conferences or refereed venues is unacceptable.
  • Following the change in this year's ICFP, we recommend (but do not require) that citations be put into author-date form (see the relevant section of the call for papers of ICFP).
